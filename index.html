Played by Jennifer Lawrence

Jennifer Lawrence was born on 15 August 1990, in Indian Hills, Kentucky, USA. Breakthrough Role: She gained widespread recognition for her role as Ree Dolly in the film "Winter's Bone" (2010), which earned her an Academy Award nomination for Best Actress. Hunger Games Franchise: Lawrence became a global superstar portraying Katniss Everdeen in the highly successful film series "The Hunger Games" (2012-2015). Academy Award Winner: She won the Academy Award for Best Actress for her performance in the film "Silver Linings Playbook" (2012). Versatile Acting Career: Lawrence has showcased her versatility in various genres, including dramas like "American Hustle" (2013), sci-fi films like "Passengers" (2016), and the superhero franchise "X-Men" (2011-2019) as Mystique. Highest-Paid Actress: In multiple years, Jennifer Lawrence was listed among the highest-paid actresses in the world due to her successful film career.

Director

Francis Lawrence

Francis Lawrence is an American filmmaker known for his work as a director and producer in the film industry. Born on March 26, 1971, in Vienna, Austria, he gained recognition for his directing skills in both music videos and feature films. His notable directorial works include directing three out of four movies in "The Hunger Games" film series: "Catching Fire" (2013), "Mockingjay – Part 1" (2014), and "Mockingjay – Part 2" (2015). He took over the franchise from Gary Ross, and his contributions helped maintain the series' success and visual style. Before his involvement in "The Hunger Games," Lawrence directed films such as "Constantine" (2005), "I Am Legend" (2007), and "Water for Elephants" (2011). He is recognized for his ability to craft visually stunning and engaging storytelling, which contributed significantly to the success of the films he directed.
